Special Olympics for Nevada

Empowering athletes with intellectual disabilities through sport, inclusion, and opportunity.

About the Special Olympics

The Special Olympics is an international sports organization dedicated to empowering individuals with intellectual disabilities through year-round sports training and athletic competition. Athletes are provided opportunities to build physical fitness, demonstrate courage, experience joy, and participate fully in their communities.

Why It Matters

The Special Olympics promotes inclusion, confidence, and equality. Through athletics, athletes improve their physical health, build friendships, and gain confidence that extends beyond sports. The program continues to positively impact individuals, families, and communities across Nevada and beyond.

Why Las Vegas Should Host the Special Olympics

Hosting the Special Olympics in Las Vegas will bring tremendous benefits to the state and city. It will attract tourism, highlight Nevada’s commitment to inclusion and community engagement, and generate positive media coverage.

  • Mayor of Las Vegas & City Council
  • Clark County Commissioners
  • Nevada Governor’s Office
  • Las Vegas Convention & Visitors Authority (LVCVA)
  • Special Olympics Nevada & Special Olympics International
