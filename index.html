About the Special Olympics

The Special Olympics is a global sports organization dedicated to empowering individuals with intellectual disabilities through year-round sports training and athletic competition. Founded on the belief that everyone deserves the chance to play, the program serves millions of athletes worldwide by fostering physical fitness, confidence, and lifelong skills.

Beyond competition, Special Olympics promotes acceptance, leadership, and inclusion—helping athletes thrive both on and off the field while strengthening communities and breaking down barriers.

Why It Matters

The Special Olympics plays a vital role in promoting equality, inclusion, and opportunity for individuals with intellectual disabilities. Participation in sports improves physical health, builds self-esteem, and encourages teamwork and leadership.

The impact extends beyond athletes—families, volunteers, and communities benefit from a culture of respect, understanding, and shared achievement that continues long after the games end.

Why Las Vegas Should Host the Special Olympics

Hosting the Special Olympics in Las Vegas would highlight Nevada’s commitment to inclusion while delivering economic and social benefits. The city’s world-class venues, accessibility infrastructure, and hospitality industry make it an ideal host location.

Bringing the games to Las Vegas would generate tourism, create volunteer opportunities, and showcase the city as a leader in community engagement and global unity.
